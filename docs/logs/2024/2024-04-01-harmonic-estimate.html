On a harmonic estimate

2024-04-01

  • Link pdf

For $m>0$, we denote by $ \mathcal{K} ^ * (\gamma, m)$ the collection of functions $f:\R^d\to \R$ such that $\Vert f\Vert_{C^{0,1}(|R^d)} \leq m$ and at every point $x\in \R^d$, we have $f\in m-C^{1,\gamma}(x)$, that is there exists $r>0$ such that

$$ f(x+h) \leq f(x) + \nabla f(x)\cdot h + m|h|^{1,\gamma}, \qquad h\in B_r(0). $$

We define $\mathcal{K}(\gamma, m)$ the collection of $C^{1,\gamma}(\R^d)$ functions with $\Vert f\Vert_{C^{1,\gamma}(\R^d)} < m$. In this note, we revisit the proof of the following estimate for harmonic functions, as found in Lemma 3.14 of 1

Proposition (Semiconcave above and $C^{1,\gamma}(x_0)$ implies growth-rate of $f\mapsto W_f$)
Let \begin{align*} w\in \mathcal{K}^*(\gamma, m)\cap \big(m-C^{1,\gamma}(x_0)\big). \end{align*} For $r\in (0,1)$, assume that $V$ satisfies \begin{equation}\label{eq:equationV} \begin{cases} \begin{aligned} \Delta V &= 0 && \text{in}\; \;\;\; D_w\cap B_r(X_0) ,\\ |V(x)| &\leq C_0|X-X_0|^{1+\gamma} && \text{on}\; \partial \big(D_w\cap B_r(X_0) \big). \end{aligned} \end{cases}\tag{1} \end{equation} Then there exists $C = C(\gamma, m, d) > 0$ so that for all $s>0$: \begin{equation*} |V(X_0+s\nu_w)| \leq CC_0s. \end{equation*}
Proof
Under the assumption that $w\in \mathcal{K}^*(\gamma, m)\cap \big(m-C^{1,\gamma}(x_0)\big)$, we can find $0< \kappa < r$ and $w^+$ such that
  • $w^+ \in \mathcal{K}(\gamma, 2m)$ with $w^+(x_0) = w(x_0)$, $\nabla w^+(x_0) = \nabla w(x_0)$ and $w(x)\leq w^+(x)$ for $x\in \R^d$.
  • For $x\in B_\kappa(x_0)$, we have $w^+(x) - w(x) \geq m|x-x_0|^{1+\gamma}$.
In particular, we have \begin{equation*} m|x-x_0|^{1+\gamma} \leq w^+(x)-w(x) \leq 3m|x-x_0|^{1+\gamma}, \qquad x\in B_\kappa(x_0). \end{equation*} Let us consider $\Phi$ solving \begin{equation*} \begin{cases} \begin{aligned} \Delta \Phi(X) &= 0 && \text{in}\;\;\;\;\; D_{w^+}\cap B_1(X_0) \\ \Phi(X) &= |X-X_0|^{1+\gamma} && \text{on}\; \partial \big( D_{w^+}\cap B_1(X_0) \big). \end{aligned} \end{cases} \end{equation*} The goal is to use $\Phi$ as a barrier on $B_{\tau}\cap D_w$ for some $\tau>0$. Note that \begin{equation*} \partial \big( D_{w}\cap B_\tau(X_0) \big) = \underbrace{\big( \Gamma_{w}\cap B_\tau (X_0) \big)}_{\Lambda_1} \cup \underbrace{\big( D_{w}\cap \partial B_\tau (X_0) \big)}_{\Lambda_2} . \end{equation*} Therefore we need to bound $\Phi$ from below on $\Lambda_1$ and $\Lambda_2$, respectively. First of all, we see that \begin{equation}\label{eq:PhiInC1GammaB12} \Phi \in C^{1,\gamma} \big( \overline{B}_{1/2}(X_0)\cap \overline{D}_{w^+} \big). \tag{2} \end{equation} Without loss of generality, let us assume $X_0 = (x_0,0) = (0,0) \in \R^{d}\times \R$. Step 1. Estimate on $\Lambda_1$ We note that if $w^+$ touches $w$ from above at $x_0$ and $w^+\in C^{1,\gamma}(\R^d)$, we can choose $w^+$ in a way such that $D_{w^+}$ at $(x_0,w^+(x_0))$ has an interior sphere condition. By Hopf Lemma at $(x_0, w^+(x_0))$ we have \begin{align*} \partial_{-e_{d+1}} \Phi(X_0) > 0. \end{align*} By continuity from $\eqref{eq:PhiInC1GammaB12}$, there exists $0<2\tau < \min\{\frac{1}{2}, \kappa\}$ small enough such that \begin{align*} \partial_{-e_{d+1}} \Phi(X) \geq \delta, \qquad X\in \Gamma_{w^+}\cap B_{2\tau}(X_0). \end{align*} Let \begin{equation*} X = (x,w(x)) \in \Gamma_w \cap B_\tau(X_0) \qquad\text{and}\qquad \widehat{X} = (x,w^+(x)). \end{equation*} We choose this way so that the (vertical) line segment connecting $X$ to $\widehat{X}$ is contained in $B_{2\tau}(X_0)\cap D_{w^+}$, so that $\partial_{-e_{d+1}} \Phi(X) \geq \delta$ here. Since $\widehat{X}_{d+1} = w^+(x) \geq w(x) = X_{d+1}$ for $x\in \R^d$, let us look at , then by the intermediate value theorem, there exists $\widetilde{X} = (x, t)$ where $t\in [w(x),w^+(x)]$ such that \begin{align*} \Phi(x,w^+(x)) - \Phi(x,w(x)) &= \partial_{e_{d+1}}\Phi(\widetilde{X})\cdot \Big(w^+(x) - w(x)\Big) \leq -\delta \Big(w^+(x) - w(x)\Big) . \end{align*} In other words, we have \begin{align*} \Phi(x, w(x)) &\geq \Phi(x,w^+(x)) + \delta \Big( w^+(x) - w(x)\Big) \\ &= |\widehat{X}-X_0|^{1+\gamma} + \delta \Big( w^+(x) - w(x)\Big) = |\widehat{X} - X_0|^{1+\gamma} + \delta |\widehat{X} - X|. \end{align*} Choose the radius $\kappa$ small such that $|\widehat{X} - X| < 1$ and thanks to $\delta \in (0,1)$, we have \begin{align*} \Phi(X) = \Phi(x, w(x)) &\geq |\widehat{X} - X_0|^{1+\gamma} + \delta |\widehat{X} - X|^{1+\gamma} \\ &\geq \delta \Big(|\widehat{X} - X_0|^{1+\gamma} + |\widehat{X} - X|^{1+\gamma}\Big) \geq \frac{\delta}{2^{1+\gamma}}|X-X_0|^{1+\gamma}. \end{align*} In other words, we have \begin{align}\label{eq:estimateLambda1} \Phi(X) \geq \frac{\delta}{2^{1+\gamma}}|X-X_0|^{1+\gamma} \qquad\text{for all}\;X\in \Lambda_1. \tag{3} \end{align} Step 2. Estimate on $\Lambda_2$ Let $Y =(x,y_{d+1}) \in \partial B_{\tau}(X_0)\cap D_{w^+}$, we want to minimize \begin{align*} \min \big\lbrace w(x) - y_{d+1}: Y = (x,y_{d+1}) \in B_{\tau}(X_0)\cap D_{w^+} \big\rbrace . \end{align*} We have \begin{align}\label{eq:estimateXHatY} |\widehat{X}Y| + |\widehat{X}X_0| \geq |X_0Y| = \tau. \tag{4} \end{align} Let $\widehat{X} = (x, \widehat{x}_{d+1}) = (x,w^+(x))$, we have \begin{align*} |\widehat{X}Y| = w^+(x) - y_{d+1} \end{align*} and, by using $|x-x_0| < 1$ if $\tau$ small enough, we have \begin{align}\label{eq:estimateXX0} |\widehat{X}X_0| = \sqrt{|x-x_0|^2 + |w^+(x)-w(x_0)|^2} \leq \sqrt{|x-x_0|^2 + m^2|x-x_0|^{2(1+\gamma)}} \leq |x-x_0|\sqrt{1+m^2}. \tag{5} \end{align} Thus, the question of interest is \begin{align*} \min \Big\lbrace |\widehat{X}Y|: Y\in \partial B_\tau(X_0)\cap D_w \Big \rbrace. \end{align*} We have the constraint \begin{align*} \tau = |X_0Y| = \sqrt{|x-x_0|^2 + |w(x_0) - y_{d+1}|^2}, \qquad Y = (x,y_{d+1})\in \partial B_{\tau}(X_0)\cap D_w. \end{align*}
  • Case 1. If $|x-x_0|\leq \frac{\tau}{2\sqrt{(1+m^2)}}$ then from \eqref{eq:estimateXX0} we have \begin{align*} |\widehat{X}X_0| \leq \frac{\tau}{2} \qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad |\widehat{X}Y|\geq \frac{\tau}{2} \end{align*} by using $\eqref{eq:estimateXHatY}$.
  • Case 2. If $|x-x_0|\geq \frac{\tau}{2\sqrt{(1+m^2)}}$ then \begin{align*} |\widehat{X}Y| = |\widehat{X}X| + |XY| \geq |\widehat{X}X| = |w^+(x) - w(x)| \geq m|x-x_0|^{1+\gamma} \geq \frac{\tau^{1+\gamma}}{(2\sqrt{2})^{1+\gamma}m^\gamma}, \end{align*} where $X = (x,w(x)) \in \Gamma_w$, and we simply estimate $|XY|\geq 0$, which happens when $Y$ is at the corner $\partial B_\tau(X_0)\cap \Gamma_w$. Note: This case we really use the fact that $Y\in \partial B_\tau(X_0)\cap D_w$ so that $|\widehat{X}X| > 0$ is strictly positive.
Combining from the two cases, we have \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \min \Big\lbrace |\widehat{X}Y|: Y\in \Lambda_2 \Big \rbrace &= \min \Big\lbrace |\widehat{X}Y|: Y\in \partial B_\tau(X_0)\cap D_w \Big \rbrace \\ &\geq C(\gamma, m) \tau^{1+\gamma} = \left(\min \left\lbrace \frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{(2\sqrt{2})^{1+\gamma}m^\gamma}\right\rbrace\right)\tau^{1+\gamma}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*} where we use $\tau \geq \tau^{1+\gamma}$. With $Y = (x,y_{d+1}) \in \partial B_\tau(X_0)\cap D_w$, $X = (x,w(x))$ and $\widehat{X} = (x, w^+(x))$, we have \begin{align*} \Phi(Y) &= \Phi(\widehat{X}) + \int_{y_{d+1}}^{w^+(x)} \partial_{-e_{d+1}} \Phi(x, t)\;dt \\ &\geq \delta \big( w^+(x) - y_{d+1} \big) \geq \delta C(\gamma, m) \tau^{1+\gamma}, \qquad Y \in \partial B_\tau(X_0)\cap D_w. \end{align*} In other words, we have \begin{align}\label{eq:estimateLambda2} \Phi(Y) \geq \delta C(\gamma, m) \tau^{1+\gamma} \qquad \text{for all}\;Y \in \Lambda_2. \tag{6} \end{align} We can assume $\delta C(\gamma, m )\tau^{1+\gamma} < 1$.
Step 3. Combine $\Lambda_1$ and $\Lambda_2$ Recall that we assume $X_0 = 0$. From the assumption on $V$ as in \eqref{eq:equationV} and \eqref{eq:estimateLambda2}, we observe that if $X\in \partial B_\tau(X_0)$, we define $X = \tau \xi$ for $\xi \in \partial B_1(X_0)$, and \begin{align*} \tilde{V}(\xi) = V(\tau \xi), \qquad \tilde{\Phi}(\xi) = \Phi(\tau \xi), \qquad \xi \in \partial B_1(0). \end{align*} On the portion of $\partial B_1(0)$ after scaling, we have \begin{align*} |\tilde{V}(\xi)| \leq C_0 \tau^{1+\gamma} \qquad\text{and}\qquad |\tilde{\Phi}(\xi)| \geq \delta C(\gamma, m) \tau^{1+\gamma}. \end{align*} Therefore \begin{equation*} |\tilde{\Phi}(\xi)| \geq \frac{\delta C(\gamma, m)}{C_0} |\tilde{V}(\xi)|. \end{equation*} In other words, we have \begin{align*} |V(X)| \leq \frac{C_0}{\delta C(\gamma, m)} |\Phi(X)| , \qquad X\in \partial B_{\tau}(X_0)\cap D_w. \end{align*} Similarly, from \eqref{eq:estimateLambda1} and \eqref{eq:equationV} on $B_\tau(X_0)\cap \Gamma_w$ we have \begin{align*} |V(X)| \leq \frac{2^{1+\gamma}C_0}{\delta} |\Phi(X)|, \qquad X\in B_{\tau}(X_0)\cap \Gamma_w. \end{align*} In other words, we found $C_1,C_2$ depending only on $\delta, \gamma, m$ such that \begin{align*} -C_1 C_0\Phi(X) \leq V(X) \leq C_2C_0\Phi(X) , \qquad x\in B_\tau(X_0)\cap D_w \end{align*} by comparison principle for harmonic function. As a consequence, thanks to $\Phi(X_0) = V(X_0) = 0$ and \begin{equation*} \Vert \Phi \Vert_{C^{1,\gamma}(\R^d)} \leq C(\gamma, m, d), \end{equation*} we have \begin{align*} |V(X_0 +s \nu_w)| \leq CC_0s \end{align*} for $s>0$ small, and $\nu_w$ is the inward normal vector toward $D_w$ at $X_0$.

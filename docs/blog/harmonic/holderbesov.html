Holder and Besov spaces: Notes and Remarks

Collections of Math notes.

Before going into the details, a useful general rule for embeddings between Besov spaces is the following.

Besov space

Let $\chi \in \mathrm{C}^\infty_c(\mathbb{R})$ be a smooth bump function such that, nonincreasing in $|s|$, with $$ \chi = 1\;\text{on}\;\left[-\frac{3}{4}, \frac{3}{4}\right], \qquad \mathrm{supp}(\chi) = \left[-\frac{4}{3}, \frac{4}{3}\right]. $$

For $s>0$, we define

$$ \begin{align}\label{eq:PaleyLittlewood-1} \varphi_{-1}(s) = \chi(s) , \qquad \varphi_0(s) = \chi\left(\frac{s}{2}\right) - \chi(s) \geq 0, \qquad \varphi_k(s) = \varphi_0\left(\frac{s}{2^k}\right) \geq 0, \quad k\geq 1. \end{align} $$

Then $\mathrm{supp}\;\varphi_k = \left[\frac{3}{4}\cdot 2^k, \frac{8}{3}\cdot 2^k\right]$ and $\sum_{k=-1}^\infty \varphi_k(s) = 1$. For $\xi \in \mathbb{R}^n$, we will, by a slight abuse of notation, write $\varphi_k(\xi) = \varphi_k(|\xi|)$.

  • If $f\in L^1(\T^n)$, the Littlewood-Paley projections $\Delta_k f\in C^\infty(\T^n)$ are defined by \begin{align}\label{eq:PaleyLittlewood-2} \Delta_k f(x) &\doteq \sum_{\xi\in \Z^n} \varphi_k(\xi) \widehat{f}(\xi)e^{2\pi i \xi \cdot x} = \sum_{ \frac{3}{4}\cdot 2^k \leq |\xi| \leq \frac{8}{3}\cdot 2^k} \varphi_k(\xi) \widehat{f}(\xi)e^{2\pi i \xi \cdot x}, \qquad x\in \T^n. \end{align}
  • If $f\in L^1(\T^n)\cap L^2(\T^n)$, by Parseval's Theorem $\Vert f\Vert_{L^2(\T^n)}^2 = \sum_{\xi\in \Z^n} |\widehat{f}(\xi)|^2$, thus $f(x) = \sum_{k=-1}^\infty \Delta_kf(x) \qquad\text{in}\;L^2(\T^n)$.

Let $K_j:\T^n\to \R$ be the inverse Fourier transform of $\varphi_j$ namely

$$ K_j(x) = \sum_{\xi\in \Z^n} \varphi_j(\xi) e^{2\pi i \xi \cdot x}, \qquad x\in \T^n, $$

where the sum is finite since $\mathrm{supp}(\varphi_j) \subset \left[\frac{3}{4}\cdot 2{j}, \frac{8}{3}\cdot 2^j\right]$.

LEMMA. $\Vert K_j\Vert_{L^1(\T^n)} \leq C$ uniformly in $j$.

Let $$ \varphi_0^\vee(x) = \int_{\R^n} \varphi_0(\xi) e^{2\pi i\xi \cdot x}\;d\xi \qquad \Longrightarrow \qquad $$

Basic identity

Theorem. Let $\alpha \in (0,1]$. We have $C^{0,\alpha}(\T^n) = B^{\alpha}_{\infty, \infty}(\T^n)$.

Proof. Let us show $C^{0,\alpha}(\T^n)\hookrightarrow B^\alpha_{\infty,\infty}(\T^n)$. From the fact that $\widehat{K_j}(0) = \varphi_j(0) = 0$ for all $j\geq 0$ we compute for $j\geq 0$ that $$ \Delta_j f(x) = (K_j*f)(x) = \int_{\T^n} K_j(y) f(x-y)\;dy = \int_{\T^n} K_j(y) \big( f(x-y) - f(x)\big) \;dy. $$ Therefore, for $j\geq 0$ we have $[f]$ $$ |\Delta_j f(x)| \leq \lbrack f \rbrack_{C^{0,\alpha}(\T^n)} \cdot 2^{-j\alpha} \left(\int_{\R^n} |\varphi_0^\vee(z)|\cdot |z|^\alpha\;dz\right). $$ Since $$ \int_{\R^n} |\varphi_0^\vee(z)|\cdot |z|^\alpha\;dz \leq \int_{\R^n} |\varphi_0^\vee(z)|\;dz \leq C_N \int_{\R^n} \frac{dz}{(1+|z|^N)}, $$ we conclude that $$ 2^{j\alpha}|\Delta_j f(x)| \leq C_N[f]_{C^{0,\alpha}(\T^n)}, \qquad j\geq 0. $$ For $j=-1$, using $\varphi_{-1} = \chi$ and $\chi(0) = 1$, we have $$ \begin{aligned} |\Delta_{-1}f(x)| &= \left| \varphi_{-1}(0)\widehat{f}(0) + \sum_{|\xi|=1} \varphi_{-1}(\xi)\widehat{f}(\xi)e^{2\pi i\xi\cdot x} \right| \\\ &\leq \sum_{|\xi|\leq 1} |\widehat{f}(\xi)|\leq (n+1)\Vert f\Vert_{L^1(\T^n)} \leq (n+1)\Vert f\Vert_{L^\infty(\T^n)}. \end{aligned} $$ We conclude that $$ \Vert f\Vert_{B^\alpha_{\infty, \infty}(\T^n)} \leq \left(C_N + \frac{n+1}{2}\right)\Vert f\Vert_{C^{0,\alpha}(\T^n)}. $$
